The Mekong River

The Barrier Between the War and Safety.

Hmong people fleeing Laos after the end of the Vietnam war didn't have an easy time of it. They were pursued and hunted by Vietnamese troops, all the while trying to keep their families safe in the jungle. Laos is one of the least densely populated countries in the world, and the countryside is not only uncharted, but also very dangerous. For those that were able to make it, the Mekong river that forms the border between Laos and Thailand still stood in the way. The crossing of the river presented a huge difficulty for the Hmong people, traveling on foot with limited supplies as they were. The only option available was to attempt swimming across, and it was about as dangerous as it sounds. Some claim that about one third of Hmong people who attempted to cross the river died in the process. I myself have met people who are permanently injured from being thrown into rocks and other debris in the river. Some had broken bones, were paralyzed, some drowned, and I even met a woman who suffered a head injury and was left deaf for the rest of her life. The difficulty of the journey became a catalyst for religious and inspirational thought. Many claim to have had supernatural help in the crossing of the river, including anything from voices to angels to ancestor spirits coming to help. In memory of this, this page will generate random inspirational quotes; something that may have occured to a Hmong refugee leaving their home.
