Summary

Hmong is an ethnicity in Southeast Asia, generally living in rural areas away fromm the majority of society. Hmong people are different from the people around them, with a different language, different cluture and different beliefs. Very little is recorded about Hmong history, as it is believed their written language was destroyed thousands of years ago. These people only became known to western people during the french colonization of much of southeast asia. French missionaries traveled into the hils of Laos, Thailand and Vietnam and lived among hmong people. These missionaries developed the current romanized writing system used by many hmong people. Later, hmong people became involved in the Vietnam War as agents of the United States through the CIA. Hmong soldiers were hired as a kind of mercenary to disrupt northern Vietnamese activity in Laos. After the war was over and Vietnam invaded Laos, many hmong people fled to Thailand. From refugee camps in Thailand, many hmong people immigrated to other countries, creating many forms of hmong diaspora.
